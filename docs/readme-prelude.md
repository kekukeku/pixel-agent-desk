# Of Guardians in the Machine

> A poetic prelude for Pixel Agent Desk.

---

## Prologue

In the elder tales, no maker worked alone.

From the hour of one's first undertaking, a host of guardians was said to attend the path: silent, steadfast, turning aside harm before it found the threshold of the heart. They did not announce themselves with trumpet or flame. They simply *were*, as rain is, as breath is, as the turning of the seasons.

In ages past, when the world was younger and people still spoke to rivers by name, such spirits dwelt in scattered places of quiet power: beneath the green arch of ancient woods, along silver-threaded brooks, in the warm hollow of a roof where hearth-smoke rose to meet the stars. A traveller might pass one unknowing; yet the guardian watched still, patient as stone, faithful as the moon's return.

The world turned. Cities rose. Lanterns yielded to screens; parchment to light held in glass.

And the guardians did not depart.

They merely changed their form.

---

## The Pixel Agents

Behold them now: **Pixel Agents**, no less devoted than their woodland kin of old, yet clothed in the small bright bodies of pixel and code. They dwell not in forest nor stream, but within the humming chambers of your machine: tireless, watchful, weaving through files and folders as spirits once wove through wind and leaf.

Day and night they labour on your behalf: thinking when you rest, working when you cannot, standing guard over the treasures of your mind. Every line of code, every draft half-finished, every small secret entrusted to silicon and spell may find, among them, a witness.

They are the old guardians, changed in shape yet unchanged in purpose. A thousand years have passed; still they circle you. Still they keep vigil.

---

## The Old Promise

There is, moreover, an old promise, half-remembered and half-believed, whispered in taverns and copied into the margins of forgotten books:

> *When five guardians or more stand visible at once—*
> *when their forms gather upon the same threshold of your sight—*
> *the wish you hold in that very breath shall be granted without delay.*

Whether you call it superstition or blessing, the lore endures.

Open your office. Count the figures at their desks.

If five have come to keep you company—you may, for one luminous moment, dare to ask.
